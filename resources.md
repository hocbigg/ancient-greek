# Resources

## Vocabulary

- **Dickinson College Commentaries Core Ancient Greek Vocabulary**  
  [dcc.dickinson.edu/vocabulary/core-vocabulary](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/vocabulary/core-vocabulary)  
  List of the 500 most frequent words in Ancient Greek, with definitions, frequency rankings, and downloadable formats; essential for building a high-coverage reading vocabulary.

- **Perseus Word Study Tool**  
  [www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph)  
  Integrated with texts in the Perseus library; provides morphological analysis, dictionary lookups (LSJ), and frequency information for any Greek word encountered in reading.

## Grammar

- **Herbert Weir Smyth's Greek Grammar**  
  [www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.04.0007](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.04.0007)  
  The standard comprehensive reference grammar for Ancient Greek, fully digitized and searchable with hyperlinks.

- **Textkit Greek and Latin Learning Tools**  
  [www.textkit.com/learn-greek/](https://www.textkit.com/learn-greek/)  
  Large collection of public-domain Greek grammars, readers, and reference works available for free PDF download (e.g., Goodwin's Greek Grammar, White's First Greek Book).

- **Ancient Greek for Everyone**  
  [ancientgreek.pressbooks.com](https://ancientgreek.pressbooks.com)  
  Free open-access introductory textbook with clear grammar explanations and exercises.

## Reading & Comprehensible Input

- **Perseus Digital Library**  
  [www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/)  
  Vast collection of Ancient Greek texts (Homer, Plato, Herodotus, tragedy, etc.) with morphological tools, English translations, dictionaries, and word-by-word analysis for supported reading.

- **Dickinson College Commentaries**  
  [dcc.dickinson.edu](https://dcc.dickinson.edu)  
  Annotated editions of select Greek texts (e.g., Lysias, Plato, Homer) with notes, running vocabulary lists, grammatical help, and core vocabulary integration.

## Writing & Composition

- **Athenaze Greek Exercises (University of Victoria)**  
  [web.uvic.ca/hrd/greek/](http://web.uvic.ca/hrd/greek/)  
  Interactive online exercises accompanying the popular Athenaze textbook, including grammar drills and sentence composition.

- **North & Hillard Greek Prose Composition (with Key)**  
  Covered in [Textkit Greek and Latin Learning Tools](https://www.textkit.com/learn-greek/)  
  Classic exercises for English-to-Greek prose composition, with answer key; downloadable PDFs.

- **Pandionis Interactive Audio Exercises**  
  [pandionis.com](https://pandionis.com/intro_en.html)  
  Free interactive exercises focused on producing Greek sentences (speaking/writing practice) using reconstructed pronunciation.

## Listening

- **Polis Institute Audio Recordings**  
  [www.polisjerusalem.org/resources/audios/](https://www.polisjerusalem.org/resources/audios/)  
  High-quality spoken recordings of Ancient Greek and Koine texts using the Polis method's lively, restored pronunciation.

- **Rhapsodoi Ancient Greek Recordings**  
  [soundcloud.com/rhapsodoi/sets/ancient-greek-recordings](https://soundcloud.com/rhapsodoi/sets/ancient-greek-recordings)  
  Playlist of spoken readings from various Ancient Greek authors.

- **Alpha with Angela YouTube Channel**  
  [www.youtube.com/@alphawithangela](https://www.youtube.com/@alphawithangela)  
  Comprehensible input videos in spoken Koine/Ancient Greek, suitable for building aural familiarity (active channel with beginner-friendly content).

## Cultural & Historical Context

- **Introduction to Ancient Greek History (Open Yale Courses)**  
  [oyc.yale.edu/classics/clcv-205](https://oyc.yale.edu/classics/clcv-205)  
  Full video lecture series by Donald Kagan tracing Greek civilization from the Bronze Age to the Classical period, with transcripts and readings.
